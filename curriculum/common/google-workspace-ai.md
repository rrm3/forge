# AI in Google Workspace

## Overview

Every Digital Science employee has access to Gemini AI features built into Google Workspace. This is the fastest path to daily AI use because you do not need to open a separate tool or learn a new interface. Gemini is already inside the Docs, Sheets, Gmail, Slides, and Meet you use every day.

This guide covers the most practical features across each app, with specific instructions you can try right now.

## Gemini: Your Core AI Assistant

Gemini is Google's AI assistant, accessible from the side panel in most Workspace apps and as a standalone chat. Think of it as a cross-app orchestrator that can pull information from your Gmail, Drive, Docs, and Sheets.

**Key capabilities to start with:**

* **Gems (Custom Assistants):** Create reusable personas for consistent output. Click Gems, then Create Gem, and define a role like "Digital Science Product Marketing Writer" or "Customer Support Drafting Assistant." Save it and reuse it every time you need that tone and style.
* **Cross-app prompts with @:** In the Gemini chat, type @ and select an app. For example: "@Gmail what was the final price from the vendor?" or "@Drive find the Q3 product roadmap." This lets you search across your workspace using natural language.
* **Deep Research Mode:** Toggle Deep Research on for complex topics. Gemini will produce a multi-page synthesized report. Useful for competitive analysis, market research, or understanding a technical domain before a meeting.

## Google Docs

Gemini in Docs helps with writing, editing, and analysis. The two most useful features:

**Help Me Write:** Click the sparkle icon on a blank line and describe what you need. "Draft a project proposal for migrating our Overleaf authentication to OIDC" or "Write a stakeholder update on the Dimensions data pipeline improvements." The output lands directly in your doc.

**Summarize This Document:** Open the Gemini side panel (top right) and click "Summarize this document." Useful when you inherit a long document and need to get up to speed quickly. Copy the summary into your own notes or share it with colleagues who do not have time to read the full thing.

**Other practical features:**
* **Proofread:** Tools then Proofread. Reviews for clarity, grammar, and flow. Accept individual suggestions or all at once.
* **Tone shifting:** Select a paragraph, then Refine, then choose Formal or Casual. Good for adapting internal notes into customer-facing language.
* **Table generation:** Ask Gemini to "create a comparison table of Dimensions vs. Scopus features" and it will produce a structured table you can insert directly.

## Gmail

AI features in Gmail help you manage communication faster.

**Summarize email threads:** Open a long chain and click "Summarize this email" at the top. Get a bulleted summary of the key points without reading every reply.

**Help Me Write:** In Compose, click the Help Me Write icon. Describe what you need: "Apologize for the delay in the Figshare implementation and provide an updated timeline." The AI drafts it; you edit and send.

**Smart Reply with context:** Open an incoming email and choose from AI-suggested replies. They are more contextual than before because Gemini understands the thread.

**Search with natural language:** In the Gemini panel, ask questions like "Find all emails from Sarah about the Altmetric renewal" or "When did we last discuss the FedRAMP timeline?" This is much faster than advanced search syntax.

**Tone polishing:** Draft a rough reply, then click Refine and Formalize. The AI cleans up grammar and adjusts the tone while keeping your meaning.

## Google Sheets

This is where AI saves the most time for anyone who works with data, which is most of us.

**Formula generation:** Open the Gemini side panel and describe the math in plain English: "Calculate the 15% tax for Column B" or "Count the number of unique institutions in Column A." Gemini generates the formula. Copy it into your cell.

**Data insights:** Click on a dataset and ask Gemini "What are the top 3 trends in this data?" It will generate charts and written analysis automatically.

**Narrative summaries:** Highlight a range of cells and ask: "Explain these results as if you are presenting to a VP who has 2 minutes." Copy the narrative into your report or presentation.

**Cleaning messy data:** Prompt: "Standardize these dates to DD/MM/YYYY" or "Remove duplicate entries based on the email column." Review the suggested fixes before applying.

**Sentiment analysis:** If you have customer feedback in a column, ask Gemini to "Categorize these as positive, negative, or neutral." It will add a labeled column.

**Python in Sheets:** Open Tools then Python. Ask Gemini to write a script for more advanced analysis, forecasting, or visualization. The script runs directly in the Sheet.

## Google Slides

**Generate slides from content:** In the Gemini side panel, prompt: "Add a slide summarizing the key findings from @[Doc Name]." It pulls from your existing documents.

**Speaker notes:** Ask Gemini to "Write 60-second speaker notes for this slide." Edit them for your speaking style.

**Image creation:** Go to Insert, then Image, then Help Me Create. Describe what you need: "A professional diagram showing the research publication lifecycle." Drag the result into your slide.

**Accessibility checking:** Ask Gemini: "Check this slide for color contrast and font size issues." Follow the recommendations to make your presentations accessible.

## Google Meet

**Take Notes for Me:** Start a meeting, click Gemini, then Take Notes for Me. After the meeting, find structured notes in your Drive. This is particularly useful for AI Tuesday sessions where you want to capture ideas.

**Catch up on meetings you join late:** Open the Gemini side panel and click "Summary so far" to see what has been discussed.

**Search past meeting transcripts:** After a recorded meeting, open the transcript and ask Gemini: "What did we decide about the timeline?" or "What action items were assigned?" Responses include timestamps.

## NotebookLM

NotebookLM is Google's research tool for working with large document collections. It is especially powerful for Digital Science because so much of our work involves dense documents.

**How it works:** Upload PDFs, docs, or paste URLs into a Notebook. Then ask questions and NotebookLM answers using ONLY the sources you provided. It will not make things up from general knowledge.

**Use cases at Digital Science:**
* Upload a set of customer RFP responses and ask: "What are the most common requirements across these documents?"
* Upload product documentation for Symplectic Elements and ask: "How does the CRIS integration work?"
* Upload competitor materials and ask: "Compare their pricing model to ours."

**Audio Overviews:** NotebookLM can generate a podcast-style audio summary of your uploaded documents. Useful for getting up to speed on a topic during a commute or walk.

## Privacy and Security Reminders

* Gemini in Google Workspace operates within our enterprise tenant. Your data is not used to train Google's models.
* Still, do not upload highly sensitive data (personal employee records, financial credentials, unredacted customer contracts) unless you have confirmed the data classification allows it.
* Check the Tooling and Security Playbook for current guidance on what data is acceptable in which tools.

## Try This Today

Pick one of these and try it right now:

1. **Gmail:** Open your longest unread email thread, click Summarize, and see if the summary captures the key points accurately.
2. **Docs:** Open a document you are working on, open the Gemini side panel, and ask it to summarize the document or suggest improvements.
3. **Sheets:** Open a spreadsheet with data, ask Gemini to identify trends, and compare its analysis to your own understanding.
4. **Create a Gem:** Go to Gemini, click Gems, and create one persona you would use regularly (e.g., "Overleaf Customer Support Writer" or "Dimensions Data Analyst").

Start with whichever app you use most. The features work best when you use them on real tasks with real data.
