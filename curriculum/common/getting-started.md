# Getting Started with AI Tuesdays

## What is AI Tuesdays?

AI Tuesdays is a 12-week initiative where every employee at Digital Science dedicates one full day per week to learning and applying AI tools. This is not a training course or a series of webinars. It is a fundamental shift in how we work, designed to re-establish Digital Science as an AI-native organization.

As Rob, our Chief AI Officer, put it: we are at an inflection point where standing still is not an option. AI-native startups and big tech platforms are entering our space, and we need to be the ones defining the industry rather than being disrupted by it.

## What AI Tuesdays Is Not

* **Not extra work.** You have the green light to deprioritize other tasks to make space for this. We are not asking you to squeeze it in on top of your current workload.
* **Not optional.** This applies to every role, every level. We do not expect perfection, but we do expect engagement.
* **Not just about tools.** It is about changing the way we solve problems and boost productivity across the whole company.
* **Not a tick-box exercise.** There is no final exam. The measure of success is whether you can do things differently and better.

## How It Works

The program runs in two phases:

**Phase 1: Learn and Engage (Weeks 1-6)**
During this phase, you will build foundational skills. You will explore AI tools, learn prompting techniques, and identify the specific tasks in your role where AI can make the biggest difference. Your functional leader will help shape learning goals for your team, and you will have access to structured learning paths.

**Phase 2: Build and Impact (Weeks 7-12)**
In the second phase, you move from learning to doing. Teams define specific problems AI could solve, build working prototypes or workflows, and validate them against real work. The goal is to come out of Week 12 with at least one concrete example of AI saving your team meaningful time.

## Your Learner Ready Checklist

Before you begin, make sure you have covered these basics:

**Required:**
* Clear your Tuesdays of all meetings and travel for the next 12 weeks
* Complete the AI Capability Survey to establish your starting level
* Set learning goals with your manager
* Review the AI safety guidelines and understand what tools are available

**Recommended:**
* Find an AI Accountability Partner (someone at a different skill level to learn alongside)
* Join the #AI-Tuesdays Slack channel
* Identify one repetitive task that eats up your time each week, as your first target

## Know Your Starting Level

Use these levels to assess where you are today. Be honest; there is no wrong answer, and knowing your starting point helps us support you.

* **Basic:** You have used ChatGPT or Gemini for simple tasks like brainstorming or drafting an email. You know AI can hallucinate but are not sure how to prevent it.
* **Intermediate:** You write multi-turn prompts, use persona-based techniques, and fact-check AI output against primary sources. You use tools like Claude, Google AI Studio, or Midjourney.
* **Advanced:** You build custom AI workflows, connect models to internal data sources, or configure AI assistants for specific business functions.
* **Expert:** You architect AI strategies, integrate models via APIs, or lead organizational AI adoption initiatives.

Most people start at Basic or Intermediate. That is completely fine. The goal is not to reach Expert by Week 12. The goal is to be an Explorer in Week 1.

## Tools Available to You

Digital Science provides access to several AI tools. The exact set available depends on your role, but here is what you should know about:

* **Gemini** (Google Workspace): Built into Docs, Sheets, Gmail, Meet, and Slides. Available to everyone.
* **Claude** (Anthropic): Strong at analysis, writing, and reasoning tasks. Available via web and API.
* **ChatGPT** (OpenAI): General-purpose assistant with broad capabilities.
* **GitHub Copilot**: AI-assisted coding for developers. Available to the Technology team.
* **Claude Code / Cursor**: AI-assisted development environments for engineers.
* **NotebookLM**: Google's research tool for analyzing large document collections.

For details on what is approved and how to handle data safely, consult the Tooling and Security Playbook.

## Safety and Privacy

Two things matter when you use AI tools: the tool you are using and the data you are putting into it.

* Never paste customer data, personal employee information, or confidential financial data into external AI tools unless that tool has been approved for that data classification.
* When in doubt, use synthetic or anonymized data for experimentation.
* If you are unsure whether something is safe to use, post a question in #AI-Tuesdays on Slack. Others will benefit from the discussion.

## How to Spend Your Time

The primary goal is to apply AI to real work tasks, not to do abstract learning for its own sake.

**Do:**
* Identify "friction points" in your weekly work: time sinks, blank-page problems, repetitive data tasks
* Try automating or augmenting a real workflow
* Collaborate with colleagues across departments
* Share what you learn in #AI-Tuesdays

**Don't:**
* Use the time for personal projects or side hustles
* Treat it as a day to catch up on regular email and BAU tasks
* Just watch tutorials without trying anything hands-on

## Getting Help

* **Your manager:** They are there to help remove blockers and create the right conditions for learning
* **Accountability Partners:** Pair up with someone to keep each other moving and share perspectives

## Try This Today

1. Open Claude or Gemini (whichever you have access to)
2. Paste in the last email you wrote that took more than 10 minutes
3. Ask the AI: "How could I have written this faster using you as a tool? Show me the workflow."
4. Read the response, then try the suggested workflow on your next email

This small exercise will give you an immediate feel for what AI can and cannot do, and it takes less than 15 minutes.
