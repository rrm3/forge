# AI for Content Creation

## Context

The Marketing team of 42 people handles product marketing, content creation, events, digital/social media, and marketing operations across all Digital Science products. The team works across B2C, Enterprise, GFN (Government, Funder, Non-profit), Publisher, and Academic segments. Content creation and repurposing is a core activity, making it one of the most obvious areas where AI can deliver quick time savings.

This guide covers practical AI workflows for the types of content the Marketing team produces daily.

## Content Drafting

### Blog Posts and Articles

AI works best as a drafting partner, not a replacement for your subject matter expertise. The most effective workflow is:

1. **Outline first.** Write your own outline with the key points, argument structure, and audience. This is the creative work that requires your marketing expertise.
2. **Draft with AI.** Give the AI your outline and ask it to expand each section:
   "Expand this outline into a 1,000-word blog post. Target audience: university research administrators considering research data management tools. Tone: professional but accessible. Use Digital Science's perspective as a provider of research infrastructure tools. Do not use words like 'transformative,' 'cutting-edge,' or 'revolutionary.' Avoid em-dashes."
3. **Edit aggressively.** AI-generated first drafts are usually 30-40% too long and too generic. Cut, sharpen, and add the specific examples and insights that only you can provide.

**Repurposing existing content:**
This is where AI really shines. Take one piece of long-form content and transform it:
"Here is a 2,000-word blog post about research data management trends. Create:
* 3 LinkedIn posts (different angles, each under 200 words)
* 2 Twitter/X posts (under 280 characters each)
* An email newsletter snippet (100 words) with a compelling subject line
* A 60-second conference pitch summarizing the key insight"

This "Content Remix" approach is the core of the Marketing team's first AI Tuesday activity.

### Case Studies

"Draft a customer case study based on these details:
* Customer: [type of institution]
* Product: Altmetric for Institutions
* Challenge: They needed to demonstrate the non-academic impact of their research to government funders
* Solution: Used Altmetric data to track policy citations, media mentions, and social media engagement
* Results: [specific metrics if available, or describe qualitative results]
Format: 600 words. Sections: Challenge, Solution, Results, Quote (draft a plausible quote from a research impact officer). Tone: factual, results-oriented, not salesy."

### Product Marketing Copy

"Write product page copy for Figshare for Institutions. Target audience: university library directors evaluating data repository solutions. They care about: compliance with funder mandates, integration with existing infrastructure, ease of administration, and researcher adoption.
Write: a headline (under 10 words), a subheadline (under 25 words), 3 feature highlights with brief descriptions, and a call-to-action. Keep total length under 200 words."

**A/B variant generation:**
"Generate 3 versions of this email subject line, each targeting a different emotional driver:
* Version 1: Curiosity
* Version 2: Urgency
* Version 3: Social proof
The email is about our upcoming webinar on AI in scholarly publishing."

## SEO and Content Strategy

### Keyword Research Acceleration

"I am writing content for the Dimensions product page targeting research administrators. Suggest:
* 10 long-tail keywords they would search for when looking for research analytics tools
* 5 questions they would type into Google about comparing research analytics platforms
* 3 content topics that could rank for these keywords
Group by search intent (informational, navigational, transactional)."

### SEO Content Optimization

"Review this blog post for SEO. The target keyword is 'institutional repository management.' Suggest:
* Where to naturally incorporate the keyword and related terms
* Improvements to the title tag and meta description
* Internal linking opportunities (we have content about Figshare, open access, and funder mandates)
* Header structure improvements for featured snippet potential"

### Content Calendar Planning

"Suggest a quarterly content calendar for Overleaf's blog. We publish 2 posts per week. Our audience is academic researchers, graduate students, and university administrators. Topics should cover:
* LaTeX tips and tutorials
* Collaboration features
* Integration with reference managers and repositories
* Open access and publishing workflows
* AI writing tools (Writefull integration)
For each suggested post, provide: title, target audience, and primary keyword."

## Brand Consistency

AI can sometimes drift toward generic marketing language. Maintaining Digital Science's voice requires explicit instructions.

**Creating a brand Gem:**
In Gemini, create a Gem with your brand guidelines:
"You are the Digital Science marketing writer. Our brand voice is: knowledgeable but not academic, helpful but not pushy, confident but not arrogant. We write for smart professionals in research and education. We avoid: buzzwords like 'innovative,' 'cutting-edge,' 'next-generation'; cliches like 'at the end of the day'; and generic marketing language like 'best-in-class' or 'industry-leading.' We prefer: specific claims with evidence, clear language, and an honest tone."

Use this Gem for all content drafting. It will save you from having to add brand guidelines to every prompt.

**Brand guideline automation:**
"Review this draft marketing email against these brand guidelines: [paste guidelines]. Identify any language that violates the guidelines and suggest replacements."

## Social Media Content

### Platform-Specific Content

Each platform has different norms. AI can help adapt a single message:

"I have this announcement: [paste]. Adapt it for:
* LinkedIn (professional, longer-form, include a thought-provoking question)
* Twitter/X (concise, punchy, include a hook)
* Email (newsletter style, with a clear CTA)
Each should feel native to the platform, not like a cross-post."

### Engagement and Community

"Generate 5 LinkedIn comment responses for posts about open access publishing. Each response should position Digital Science as a thoughtful participant in the conversation (not self-promotional). Reference our work with Figshare and Overleaf where natural."

### Event Promotion

"Create a social media campaign plan for Digital Science's presence at [conference name]. We are:
* Exhibiting on the show floor
* Presenting a session on [topic]
* Hosting a networking event on [date]
Create: 3 pre-event posts (building anticipation), 3 during-event posts (live updates), and 2 post-event posts (recap and follow-up). Include suggested hashtags."

## Campaign Analysis

### Performance Reporting

AI can help interpret marketing data and generate narrative reports.

"Here are this month's email campaign metrics: [paste metrics table]. Analyze:
* Which campaigns performed above/below benchmarks?
* What trends do you see in open rates and click-through rates?
* Which segments are responding best?
* What should we test or change next month?
Write a brief (one-page) performance summary for the marketing leadership meeting."

### Customer Persona Refinement

"Based on this data about who engages with our Altmetric content: [paste engagement data by segment, role, institution type]. Refine our marketing personas. What patterns do you see? Are there segments we are over-investing in or under-serving?"

## Translation and Localization

Digital Science markets globally. AI can help with initial translations and cultural adaptation, though professional translation should be used for published materials.

"Translate this marketing email into German, maintaining the professional tone. Note: our German academic audience tends to prefer more formal language than our UK audience. Adjust accordingly."

"Adapt this US-focused case study for a UK university audience. Change relevant terminology (e.g., 'grants' vs. 'funding councils'), currency, and institutional context."

## Try This Today

1. Find one piece of long-form content your team published in the last month (blog post, whitepaper, webinar transcript)
2. Ask AI to remix it into 5 different formats: 3 social media posts, an email snippet, and a conference talk pitch
3. Review each output. Rate them 1-5 for accuracy, tone, and usefulness.
4. Edit the best ones until they are publish-ready
5. Calculate: How long would this have taken manually? How long did it take with AI?
6. Share your remixed content and time savings in #AI-Tuesdays

This is exactly the "Content Remix Workshop" that is the Marketing team's first AI Tuesday activity. Bring a real piece of content and transform it.
