# Meeting Prep with AI

## Context

Sales professionals spend significant time preparing for customer meetings, debriefing afterward, and managing follow-ups. AI tools can cut this preparation time while improving the quality of your briefings. This guide walks through the complete meeting lifecycle: before, during, and after.

## Before the Meeting: Research and Briefing

### The 15-Minute Meeting Prep

For most customer meetings, you can prepare a solid briefing in 15 minutes using AI. Here is the workflow:

**Step 1: Gather what you know (3 minutes)**
Pull up the account in Salesforce. Note: last interaction date, deal stage, key contacts, any open issues, and the stated agenda for this meeting.

**Step 2: Research the institution (5 minutes)**
Ask AI:
"I have a meeting with [name and title] at [institution]. They are a [current/prospective] customer for [product]. Here is what I know: [paste CRM summary].

Prepare a meeting briefing with:
* Recent news about [institution] relevant to research infrastructure or publishing
* Their likely priorities given their role and institution type
* 3 discovery questions I should ask
* Potential objections they might raise and how to address them"

**Step 3: Tailor your talking points (5 minutes)**
Based on the AI research and your CRM data, draft your key talking points. Ask AI:
"Based on this briefing, suggest how I should position [Overleaf/Dimensions/Altmetric/Elements] for this customer. What feature or value proposition should I lead with?"

**Step 4: Prepare a one-pager (2 minutes)**
If you need a leave-behind or a summary to share with the customer:
"Create a one-page summary of how [product] addresses [institution type] needs. Include 3 key benefits and one relevant case study or proof point. Format as a clean document I can share via email."

### Renewal Meeting Prep

Renewal conversations require specific preparation:

"I have a renewal conversation with [customer]. Their current contract: [product, term, value, user count]. Their usage over the past year: [paste usage data if available]. Upcoming contract end date: [date].

Help me prepare:
* Summary of value delivered during the current term
* Talking points for why renewal makes sense
* How to handle a potential price sensitivity discussion
* Upsell opportunities based on their usage patterns"

### New Business Discovery Call Prep

For first meetings with prospects:

"I have a first discovery call with [title] at [institution]. They expressed interest in [product] through [channel: website inquiry, conference, referral].

Help me prepare:
* Background on the institution and their research profile
* 5 open-ended discovery questions to understand their needs
* Common needs that institutions like this typically have
* A 60-second elevator pitch for [product] tailored to their likely interests"

## During the Meeting: Real-Time Support

### Using Gong

Gong records and analyzes your calls. After AI Tuesdays, consider how to get more from Gong's AI features:

* Review Gong's automatic call summaries after each meeting
* Look at the talk-to-listen ratio. Are you talking too much?
* Search past Gong recordings: "Find all calls where [competitor name] was mentioned" to understand competitive positioning patterns
* Review successful calls with similar customers to prepare for upcoming ones

### Quick Reference During Calls

If you need to look something up during a live call:
* Have Gemini open in a browser tab
* Ask quick questions: "What is the difference between Figshare's institutional and individual plans?" or "What integrations does Symplectic Elements support?"
* This is faster than searching documentation

Note: Do not rely on AI for answers you should know. Use it as a backup for edge cases and detailed technical questions that come up unexpectedly.

## After the Meeting: Follow-Up

### Meeting Summary

Within 30 minutes of the meeting, while details are fresh:

If you have a Gong recording or Google Meet transcript:
"Summarize this meeting transcript. Create three sections:
* Key takeaways (what did we learn about the customer's needs?)
* Action items (who committed to doing what, by when?)
* Next steps (what happens next in this deal?)
Keep each section to 3-5 bullet points."

If you have your own notes:
"Here are my rough meeting notes: [paste]. Turn these into a clean meeting summary I can log in Salesforce and share with the account team."

### Follow-Up Email

The follow-up email is critical. Send it within 24 hours while the meeting is still fresh.

"Draft a follow-up email to [name] after our meeting today. Reference:
* Their interest in [specific feature or capability]
* The [specific pain point] they mentioned
* Action items: [list what you and they committed to]
* Propose a next step: [demo, pilot, meeting with technical team, etc.]
Tone: professional, personalized, and forward-moving. Under 200 words."

### CRM Update

Use AI to help write concise Salesforce notes:

"Based on these meeting notes, write a concise CRM entry (under 100 words) that captures: current deal status, customer's key concerns, next steps, and any changes to deal timeline or value."

### Internal Briefing

If you need to brief colleagues (pre-sales, implementation, leadership) about a customer meeting:

"Write a brief internal update for our account team about today's meeting with [customer]. They need to know: what the customer wants, what we committed to, and what support I need from them. Keep it action-oriented."

## Building Your Meeting Prep System

The most efficient sales professionals build reusable templates. Here is a system:

**Create a "Meeting Prep" Gem in Gemini:**
Define it with your standard briefing format, your product knowledge, and your preferred output style. Every time you prep for a meeting, use this Gem instead of writing a new prompt from scratch.

**Maintain a prompt library in a Google Doc:**
* Discovery call prep prompt
* Renewal meeting prep prompt
* QBR prep prompt
* Follow-up email prompt
* CRM update prompt
* Internal briefing prompt

Refine these each week as you learn what works.

**Weekly review:**
At the end of each week, review the meetings you used AI to prep for. What worked? What did AI get wrong? Where did you add the most value manually? Use these observations to improve your prompts.

## Common Pitfalls

* **Over-relying on AI research:** AI may have outdated information about an institution. Cross-reference with your CRM, LinkedIn, and the institution's website.
* **Generic follow-ups:** AI-generated follow-up emails need personal touches. Reference something specific the customer said that only you would know.
* **Ignoring body language and tone:** AI cannot capture the subtext of a conversation. Your own judgment about a customer's enthusiasm, hesitation, or frustration is more valuable than any transcript summary.
* **Not verifying product claims:** If AI suggests a feature or integration that you are not sure about, check with the product team before telling the customer.
