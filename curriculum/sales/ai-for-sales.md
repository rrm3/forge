# AI for Sales

## Context

The Sales team of 75 people manages relationships across academic, publisher, enterprise, and government/funder segments, selling Overleaf, Altmetric, Figshare, Dimensions, IFI Claims, and Metaphacts. The team uses Salesforce CRM, Gong for call recording, and standard sales tooling. AI tools work through the interfaces you already know, so there is no need to learn coding or technical platforms.

This guide covers practical AI workflows for prospecting, account research, proposal writing, and day-to-day sales productivity.

## Account Research

Before every customer interaction, you need to understand who you are talking to. AI can compress hours of research into minutes.

**Institution research:**
Open Claude or Gemini and prompt:
"Research [University of Melbourne / Merck / ARPA-H]. I am selling research infrastructure products (publication tools, research data management, research analytics). Tell me:
* Their size, research focus areas, and recent strategic priorities
* Any recent news about their research infrastructure or technology investments
* Likely pain points with research management and publishing workflows
* Who typically makes purchasing decisions for research tools at this type of institution"

**Account briefing before a call:**
"I have a call with [customer name] at [institution]. Here is what I know about them from our CRM: [paste relevant Salesforce notes]. Based on this history, prepare a one-page briefing covering: their likely current priorities, questions I should ask, and which of our products (Overleaf, Figshare, Altmetric, Dimensions, Symplectic Elements) would be most relevant to discuss."

**Competitive intelligence:**
"Compare Digital Science's Dimensions product against [competitor product] for a large research university evaluating research analytics platforms. What are our strengths? What objections might the customer raise? How should I position Dimensions?"

**Important caveat:** AI generates plausible research, but it may include outdated or incorrect information. Always verify key facts (contract dates, specific personnel, budget numbers) against your CRM data and direct customer conversations.

## Prospecting and Outreach

AI can help you personalize outreach at a scale that would be impossible manually.

**Personalized first touch:**
1. Research the prospect's institution and their role
2. Prompt: "Write a brief, personalized cold email to [title] at [institution]. They manage research data infrastructure. I want to introduce Figshare for Institutions. Reference a recent development at their university (their new data management policy / recent large grant award / expansion of their research program). Keep it under 100 words. Do not be salesy. Sound like a colleague, not a vendor."
3. Review and personalize further. Add any specific reference you know.

**LinkedIn outreach:**
"Draft a LinkedIn connection request message for [title] at [institution]. I want to start a conversation about how they manage research output data. Keep it to 2 sentences. No hard sell."

**Follow-up sequences:**
"Write a series of 3 follow-up emails spaced one week apart for a prospect who has not responded to my initial outreach about Altmetric. Each email should add new value (not just 'checking in'). First email should share a relevant use case, second should reference a trend in research impact measurement, third should offer a specific 15-minute demo."

**Batch personalization:**
If you have a list of 20 prospects, give AI the list with their titles and institutions, and ask it to generate a personalized opening line for each. This is much faster than writing each from scratch but still requires your review.

## Proposal and RFP Responses

Proposal writing is one of the biggest time investments in enterprise sales. AI can cut the drafting time significantly.

**First draft generation:**
"Draft a proposal for [institution]'s evaluation of Overleaf for their university. They have 15,000 researchers across 12 faculties. They need: collaborative writing tools, integration with their institutional repository (Figshare), and support for LaTeX and Rich Text authors. Include sections on: Solution Overview, Implementation Approach, Support Model, Pricing Framework (placeholder), and Why Digital Science."

**RFP responses:**
Many RFP responses involve answering the same or similar questions across different bids.
1. Build a master document of your best answers to common RFP questions (security, compliance, integrations, support SLAs, company background)
2. For each new RFP, ask AI: "Here is the RFP question: [paste]. Here is our standard response: [paste]. Tailor the response to match this RFP's specific language and requirements while maintaining accuracy."
3. Review for accuracy. RFP responses become contractual, so every claim must be true.

**Proposal review:**
Before submitting, ask AI: "Review this proposal from the perspective of a university procurement officer. What questions or concerns would they have? What is weak or unclear?"

## Email and Communication

**Drafting routine emails faster:**
* "Draft an email to [customer] confirming our renewal discussion. Confirm the renewal price of [X], the term of [Y years], and the next step of scheduling the IT security review. Professional tone."
* "Write a thank-you email after today's demo with [institution]. Reference their interest in [specific feature]. Suggest a follow-up meeting to discuss implementation details."

**Handling objections in writing:**
"A customer has raised this objection: [paste the exact objection]. Draft a response that acknowledges their concern, provides a clear answer, and redirects to the value proposition. Keep it under 100 words."

**Summarizing long email threads:**
Use Gemini in Gmail to summarize threads before responding, or paste the thread into an AI tool: "Summarize this email thread between our team and [customer]. What is the current status? What has been agreed? What is still open?"

## Pipeline and Deal Management

**Deal strategy:**
"I am working a deal with [institution type] for [product]. Here is the current status: [describe stage, stakeholders, competition, timeline]. What are the 3 most important things I should do this week to advance this deal? What risks should I watch for?"

**Objection preparation:**
"List the 5 most common objections a research university raises when evaluating [product]. For each objection, provide a brief, honest response that addresses the concern while reinforcing our value."

**Win/loss analysis:**
After a deal closes (won or lost), ask AI: "Based on this summary of the deal, what were the key factors that led to this outcome? What could we have done differently? What should we learn for future similar deals?"

## Understanding Our AI Products

As Digital Science builds more AI-powered features into our products, the Sales team needs to understand and articulate these capabilities to customers.

**Products with AI features:**
* **Writefull:** AI-powered writing tools for academic authors (language editing, paraphrasing, text generation). Part of Overleaf.
* **ReadCube AI Assistant:** AI features for literature management and research workflows.
* **Attention Digest:** AI-generated summaries of attention data from Altmetric.
* **Dimensions:** Exploring natural language interfaces for research data queries.

**Learning exercise:** Use each of these AI features yourself. The best way to sell an AI product is to be a genuine user who can share real experiences.

## Data Privacy Reminders for Sales

* Never paste real customer contract terms, pricing, or personal contact details into external AI tools
* Anonymize customer data when using AI for analysis or practice
* When discussing AI features with customers, be clear about what our products do and do not do with their data
* Refer customers to our data processing and privacy documentation for detailed questions
