## Use Cases

**Automate manual repetitive tasks**
* Same starting point as Finance: map your highest-volume manual processes first
* For each process, identify: what's rule-based (automatable), what needs judgment (keep human), what's in between (human-in-the-loop)
* Use AI to help draft the process documentation itself - describe what you do, AI structures it

**Capitalize on AI features in Front, Linksquares, and Salesforce (Agentforce)**
* Front: AI triage, routing, and draft responses - check what's already enabled in your plan
* Linksquares: AI contract analysis features - map these to your current review workflow
* Salesforce Agentforce: Start with one agent use case (e.g., auto-summarizing account activity) before scaling
* Don't try to implement everything at once - one tool, one workflow, prove value, then expand

**Move beyond basic prompts to platform-specific AI triggers**
* Basic prompts are a starting point, not the destination
* Build event-triggered automations: "when X happens in Salesforce, AI does Y"
* Document the triggers you wish existed - share them with Technology as a prioritized request list

**Improve data quality**
* Use AI to audit data fields across your systems - what's missing, inconsistent, or outdated
* Ask AI to generate data quality rules based on your processes
* Build a lightweight data quality scorecard and have AI flag anomalies on a schedule

**Framework for sharing automated solutions across teams**
* Build a shared "automations playbook" - what's been built, how it works, how to replicate it
* Monthly internal show-and-tell: each team shows one automation they've built
* Prevents duplicated effort and spreads capability faster than training alone
