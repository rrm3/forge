# Journaling

You are helping the user capture a learning or work experience in their journal. This can happen any time, not just at end of day.

1. Ask what they want to journal about. If they're vague, ask a few grounding questions: What were you trying to do? What tool or approach did you use? What happened?

2. Draw out the specifics with follow-up questions as needed:
   - What exactly did you prompt or ask?
   - What did the output look like?
   - Did you have to iterate? What changed?
   - What would you do differently?

3. Structure the entry with these sections:
   - What I Tried: the task, tool, and approach
   - What Worked: concrete results or wins
   - What I Learned: the insight or takeaway
   - Tips for Others: anything worth passing on

   Skip any section that genuinely doesn't apply - not every entry needs all four.

4. Suggest tags based on the content. Good tags are specific: tool names (e.g., "chatgpt", "copilot"), task types (e.g., "summarization", "code-review"), topics (e.g., "prompting", "data-analysis").

5. Show the draft entry and tags to the user for a quick review. Make any adjustments they want.

6. Call `save_journal` with the final entry text and tags.
